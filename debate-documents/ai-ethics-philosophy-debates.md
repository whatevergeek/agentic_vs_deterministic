# AI Ethics & Philosophy Debates: Agentic AI vs. Deterministic AI Workflows

**The Core Distinction:** Both approaches leverage AI intelligence, but differ in control paradigms:
- **Agentic AI Workflow**: Give AI a goal ‚Üí Let AI determine its own path to achieve it
- **Deterministic AI Workflow**: Give AI a goal + prescribed steps ‚Üí AI follows controlled path

---

## 1. Moral Agency: AI-Guided Human Responsibility vs. AI-Distributed Decision-Making ü§î

**Ethical Scenario:**  
An AI-powered investment system makes a decision that results in significant financial losses for pension funds, affecting thousands of retirees' futures. Who bears moral responsibility?

**The Tension:**  
**Deterministic AI Workflow**: AI analyzes investment options following prescribed ethical guidelines and risk parameters, but humans make the final investment decisions at critical points, preserving clear moral accountability and human agency.

**Agentic AI Workflow**: AI autonomously weighs ethical considerations, risk factors, and investment opportunities to make decisions, distributing moral responsibility between human oversight and AI reasoning, potentially more efficient but blurring accountability lines.

**No Clear Winner:**  
Should moral responsibility remain clearly with humans through AI-guided deterministic control, or can ethical decision-making be shared between humans and autonomous AI agents?

**Polling Question:**  
"For moral responsibility in AI systems, maintain: **AI-guided clear human decision authority** or **AI-distributed shared human-AI decision-making**?"
